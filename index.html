Reading & Language Arts

Henry Adams once noted that the study of narrative perspective requires a deep understanding of historical rhetoric. His analysis of 19th-century prose provides a primary example of how persuasive language can shift public opinion.

Next, we will look at "Supporting Details." These are the facts that help prove the main idea is true. For example, if the story is about how dogs make great pets, a supporting detail might be that dogs are very loyal to their owners.
